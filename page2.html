Military History

Napoleon had a long and successful military career with many victories underneath his belt.

Here are 2 epic battles that he fought and won with details behind the battles.

Siege of Toulon

The Siege of Toulon happened in 1793 and was Napoloens first victory in his miliary carrer. It was fought between Republican forces and Royalist rebels during the French Revolution. It was this battle in which Napoleon made his mark and gained reputation as being an excellent commander. His strategy to recapture key naval bases at Toulon led to the siege swinging in the French Republics favour which allowed them to challenge the British navy and start to recapture the city.

Battle of Austerlitz

The Battle of Austerlitz occured on the 2nd of December 1805 and fighting againast Napoleon were Russian forces led by Emperor Alexander the first and Austrian forces led by Holy Roman Emperor Francis the second. The reason this battle is so interesting is because Napoleon was fighting against 2 armies that together formed a larger force than he commanded with Napoleon having 65,000-75,000 troops and the opposing army being 85,000-95,000 strong. Napoleon used his elite strategy to defeat them and is remembered as one of the most impressive military victories of that era due to odds Napoleon was up against
